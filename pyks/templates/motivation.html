Current main stream key servers like SKS - Synchronizing Key Server or PKS - Public Key Servers also synchronize their databases so it doesn't matter which concrete keyserver you connect to they all have the same data.

While this seems to be all you have to expect from a key server at a first look there are some drawbacks with the current Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) and the OpenPGP Web-of-Trust (WoT):

Harvesting e-mail addresses

Your public key certificate contains information about your identity, i.e your e-mail address and usually your full name. The distribution of your public key certificate accross a global network of synchronized keyservers distributes your e-mail address accross the internet. This make it possible for Spammers etc. to harvest those data. An empirical evaluation of this kind of threat you can find in the pgp-keyserver article at spamschlucker.org.

Social network graph

Your public key certificate contains information about those people that have signed your key. With this information (at least their pulblic key IDs) one can determine their identities (e-mail address plus their real names). Furthermore anyone who is able to read/record your internet traffic could also analyze your traffic from and to a public key server. With this information one can build a social network graph that allows the computation of your direct contacts and the people you know only indirect via persons you know and so on. That can information can be used to collect communication meta data of all people a surveiled target person might know.

Authenticity

How do you know that the public key of 'your.friend@example.org' does belong to your friend? Can you be sure that e-mail address was not set up from an adversatary?
